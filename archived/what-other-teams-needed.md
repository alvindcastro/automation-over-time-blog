---
title: "What Other Teams Actually Needed"
description: "Learning that automation meant different things to different groups."
pubDate: 2025-11-03
---

Early conversations revealed a gap.

Different teams used the same underlying systems, but their priorities weren’t identical. What counted as a critical workflow in one area was a background concern in another.

Some teams cared most about data integrity. Others focused on user‑facing processes. Risk tolerance varied. So did expectations around failure.

It became clear that “automated testing” was too vague a phrase.

Listening mattered more than explaining.

Instead of leading with what automation could do, discussions centered on where friction existed and what confidence looked like for each group.

Those distinctions shaped what followed.

Shared capability would only work if it adapted to different needs without pretending they were the same.
