---
title: "Drawing the First Boundaries"
description: "Defining what automation would and would not be responsible for."
pubDate: 2025-08-01
---

Boundaries arrived quietly.

Not in the form of architecture diagrams or policy documents, but through repeated decisions to *not* automate certain things.

Some checks remained manual by design. Others were deferred intentionally. Not everything benefited from automation.

These decisions weren’t about capability. They were about trust.

Automation needed to be predictable before it could be useful. Clear limits made behavior easier to reason about.

Saying “no” early prevented future disappointment.

That clarity didn’t slow progress.

It made progress sustainable.

Phase 2 ended not with more automation but with better automation.
