---
title: "Where Boundaries Held"
description: "Why clear limits preserved reliability."
pubDate: 2025-09-08
---

As the system matured, pressure to expand never fully disappeared.

There were always more scenarios that could be automated. More coverage that could be added. More paths that looked tempting.

Boundaries mattered more here than earlier.

Some checks remained manual. Others were deliberately excluded. These decisions weren’t about technical limitation - they were about responsibility.

Automation was trusted because its scope was understood.

Clear limits made failures easier to interpret and successes more meaningful.

Most importantly, boundaries prevented automation from making promises it couldn’t keep.

Reliability depended as much on what automation did *not* do as on what it did.
