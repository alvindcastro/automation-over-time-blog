title: "Automation Through a Leadership Lens"
description: "Understanding how automation is evaluated outside the build team."
pubDate: 2026-01-05
---

The audience changed the conversation.

For those building automation, value often appeared as coverage, speed, or technical elegance. Leadership is evaluated through a different lens.

Predictability mattered more than breadth. Reliability mattered more than novelty.

Questions centered on risk, confidence, and long‑term sustainability. Automation wasn’t viewed as a technical achievement, but as a mechanism for reducing uncertainty during change.

This reframing was helpful.

It clarified which aspects of the work mattered most at an organizational level and which were secondary. It also reinforced earlier decisions to prioritize boring behavior over clever solutions.

Automation that aligned with this perspective didn’t need additional explanation.

Its purpose was already clear.
