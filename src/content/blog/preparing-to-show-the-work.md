---
title: "Preparing to Show the Work"
description: "Deciding what to demonstrate — and what to leave out."
pubDate: 2025-12-15
---

Preparation mattered more than the demo itself.

Showing automation wasn’t about proving capability. It was about choosing which behaviors represented the work honestly and which details would distract from its intent.

There was a temptation to polish. To streamline paths. To highlight best‑case outcomes.

That temptation was resisted.

Instead, preparation focused on normal behavior. The routines. The checks that ran quietly after every change. The places where automation reduced uncertainty rather than added spectacle.

This required omission as much as inclusion.

What wasn’t demonstrated sent as strong a signal as what was. The goal wasn’t to impress. It was to communicate how the system behaved when things were ordinary.

That framing shaped everything that followed.
