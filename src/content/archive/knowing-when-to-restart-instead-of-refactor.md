The reset created space, but it didn’t answer everything.

Starting again required making deliberate choices about what to leave behind. Not just tools or code, but assumptions that are no longer held up under scrutiny.

The earlier attempt had taught a clear lesson: carrying forward a fragile structure only speeded up failure. Rebuilding meant being selective.

This restart wasn’t about solving the whole problem at once. It was about choosing a narrow entry point that could support change without collapsing under it.

Automation resumed with fewer goals and tighter boundaries. Coverage was no longer the priority. Stability was.

Progress slowed, but confidence increased.

That trade‑off turned out to be worth it.
